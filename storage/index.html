    General Intro to Web development: Storage, Bandwidths and Backups

    by Karlie Morey

    image design by © Karlie Morey

    Web Development

    Understanding Web development. Web development is the process of constructing and maintaining a website. It also consists of all the small details and fine tuning of a website that makes it function and look great. There are two parts that make up the web development field. Those two fields are called the front-end devs and the back-end devs. The “front-end” refers to the user interface side and the “back-end” is the server side. The “front-end” is what you see and interact with. Such as the text, dropdown menus and over all, all visual aspects. The “back-end” devs are what runs the website and what keeps it functioning and running. Which consists of all the data, data storage, and programs that make the website up to date and efficient.

    Storage

    What is storage? Storage is basically the amount of data that is and can be stored on your website. To store data on the internet you will need a server. Servers are what help us store data on the internet. The capacity of said servers will tell you how much storage you have. However, storage is not an infinite thing. This is because servers are physical objects that take up space and have a certain capacity for running numbers.

    Bandwidth

    Bandwidth and storage are often confused for one another. Bandwidth is the connectivity of servers and data. It also relatively refers to the amount of data transferred. To be more specific bandwidth can also be referred to as the measured amount of data being sent back and forth from website to server. A good example of bandwidth is traffic. Imagine there are 3,300 cars that need to go down the same road to get to their destination. A road needs to be built accordingly. Therefore the road should be built so that it can maintain 4,000 cars to be more efficient and avoid the risk of any possible traffic jams.

    Backups

    When important documents or important information that is copied and stored apart from its original copy. It is to ensure that the information won't get lost. This is the process of a backup. There are different types of backups. One being a Full backup. A full backup refers to copying everything that is deemed important. And is generally the copy of the first most reliable document. The second type of backup is an incremental backup. This type of backup will save space and time by detecting that all the files in the full backup will remain the same. The third and final type of backup is the Differential backup. The differential backup is almost identical to the incremental file. Except with the differential backup, all the files created since the original full backup will always be copied again.
